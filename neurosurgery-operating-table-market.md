<p><p>Neurosurgery operating tables can be categorized into two main types: motorized and non-motorized. Motorized tables are equipped with powered mechanisms that allow for easy adjustment of height, tilt, and positioning of the patient during surgery. These tables are often favored for their precision and convenience. On the other hand, non-motorized tables rely on manual adjustments by the surgical team. While they may require more effort to maneuver, non-motorized tables are typically more cost-effective and simple to operate.</p></p>
