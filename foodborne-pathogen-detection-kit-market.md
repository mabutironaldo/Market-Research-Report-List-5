<p><p>Foodborne pathogen detection kits are essential tools for ensuring food safety by detecting harmful pathogens in food products. There are two main types of detection kits widely used in the market: PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) and ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay). </p><p>PCR kits work by amplifying and detecting DNA sequences specific to the target pathogen, providing highly sensitive results. ELISA kits, on the other hand, detect pathogens through antibody-antigen interactions, offering rapid and accurate detection. Both types of kits play a crucial role in preventing foodborne illnesses and ensuring the safety of the food supply chain.</p></p>
