<p><p>Transmucosal drug delivery involves administering drugs through mucous membranes such as the mouth, nose, and rectum. Passive transmucosal drug delivery relies on the drug's ability to pass through the mucosal membrane without the need for additional mechanisms, while active transmucosal drug delivery involves the use of techniques such as permeation enhancers, microneedles, and iontophoresis to enhance drug absorption. Both types of drug delivery methods offer benefits such as improved bioavailability, rapid onset of action, and reduced systemic side effects.</p></p>
