<p><ul><li>Fast-acting</li><li>Intermediate-acting</li><li>Long-acting</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Insulin preparations are categorized into three main types based on their duration of action: fast-acting, intermediate-acting, and long-acting. Fast-acting insulins typically start working within 15 minutes, peak in about an hour, and last for 2-4 hours. Intermediate-acting insulins have a slower onset, peak in 4-12 hours, and last for up to 24 hours. Long-acting insulins have a slow and steady release over 24 hours, providing a consistent level of insulin in the body. Each type of insulin is tailored to meet the individual needs of patients with diabetes.</p></p>
