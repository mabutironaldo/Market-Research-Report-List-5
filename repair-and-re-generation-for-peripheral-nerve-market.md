<p><p>Repair and regeneration for peripheral nerve injuries can be achieved through different market types such as direct repair, nerve grafting, nerve conduit, and other specialized treatments. Direct repair involves stitching the damaged nerve ends together, while nerve grafting involves using a section of nerve from another part of the body to bridge the gap. Nerve conduits are hollow tubes that guide nerve fibers to repair the injury. Other markets include stem cell therapy, growth factors, and biomaterials to enhance nerve regeneration. Each method offers unique benefits and considerations for treating peripheral nerve injuries.</p></p>
