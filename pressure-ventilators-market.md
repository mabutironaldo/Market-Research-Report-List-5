Positive pressure ventilators work by increasing the pressure in the patient's airways to push oxygen into the lungs, whereas negative pressure ventilators work by creating a vacuum around the chest to expand the lungs and draw in air. Positive pressure ventilators are more commonly used in clinical settings, while negative pressure ventilators are typically reserved for specialized cases.</p></p>
