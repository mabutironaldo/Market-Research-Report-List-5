<p><p>Edge Emitting Lasers (EEL) are a type of semiconductor laser diode that emit light from the edge of the chip. The main types of EELs include Distributed Feedback Laser, Distributed Bragg Reflector Laser, Fabry-Perot Laser, and Broad Area Laser Diodes. Distributed Feedback Lasers have a grating structure for single-mode operation, Distributed Bragg Reflector Lasers use a Bragg grating for high-performance applications, Fabry-Perot Lasers have two reflective facets for multi-mode operation, and Broad Area Laser Diodes have a wide emitting area for high-power applications.</p></p>
