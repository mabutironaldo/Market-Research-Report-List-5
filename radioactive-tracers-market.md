<p><p>Radioactive tracers are commonly used in medical imaging such as Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT). PET tracers involve injecting a small amount of radioactive substance into the body to track metabolic processes and diagnose conditions like cancer. SPECT tracers work similarly but focus on blood flow and tissue function. Both technologies are non-invasive and provide detailed images for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning in various medical fields.</p></p>
