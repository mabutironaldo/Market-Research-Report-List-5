<p><p>Forestry tractors can be classified into two main types based on their drive system: Four-Wheel Drive and Two-Wheel Drive. Four-Wheel Drive forestry tractors are equipped with all-wheel drive capability, providing better traction and performance in rough terrains and challenging conditions. On the other hand, Two-Wheel Drive tractors have power only to the rear wheels, making them more suitable for less demanding forest operations. Both types of tractors have their advantages and are chosen based on the specific needs and requirements of forestry tasks.</p></p>
