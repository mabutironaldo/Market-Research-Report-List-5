<p><p>Navigation Satellite Systems are categorized into three main types: Global Constellations, Regional Constellations, and Satellite-Based Augmentations. Global Constellations, such as GPS and GLONASS, provide worldwide coverage and are used for various applications. Regional Constellations, like BeiDou and Galileo, offer coverage in specific regions and can complement global systems. Satellite-Based Augmentations enhance the accuracy and reliability of existing systems, such as WAAS and EGNOS. Each type serves a specific purpose in improving navigation and positioning capabilities across the globe.</p></p>
